## Why Tax Planning Matters

Income tax is one of the largest annual outflows for a salaried professional. Strategic tax planning — legally minimising your tax liability using available deductions — can save ₹30,000 to ₹1,00,000+ per year depending on your income and investments.

Tax planning should happen year-round, not just in January–March.

---

## New vs Old Tax Regime (AY 2025-26 onwards)

India now has two tax regimes:

**New Regime (default from FY 2023-24)**:
- Lower tax rates
- No deductions available (no 80C, 80D, HRA, etc.)
- Standard deduction of ₹75,000 for salaried employees
- Better for those with fewer deductions

**Old Regime**:
- Higher tax rates
- All deductions available (80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, etc.)
- Better for those with high Section 80C investments and rent

**Which is better?**: Calculate both. As a rule of thumb, if your total deductions exceed ₹3.75 lakh, the old regime likely saves more tax.

## Old Regime Tax Slabs (FY 2025-26)

| Income | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹2.5 lakh | Nil |
| ₹2.5L – ₹5L | 5% |
| ₹5L – ₹10L | 20% |
| Above ₹10L | 30% |

Plus 4% health and education cess on total tax.

---

## New Regime Tax Slabs (FY 2025-26)

| Income | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹3 lakh | Nil |
| ₹3L – ₹7L | 5% |
| ₹7L – ₹10L | 10% |
| ₹10L – ₹12L | 15% |
| ₹12L – ₹15L | 20% |
| Above ₹15L | 30% |

Note: Income up to ₹7 lakh has zero tax under new regime due to rebate u/s 87A.

## Section 80C — ₹1.5 Lakh Deduction

The maximum 80C investment is ₹1.5 lakh per financial year. This covers:
- **EPF**: Automatically deducted from salary (employee contribution of 12% of basic)
- **PPF**: Up to ₹1.5 lakh annually. Tax-free Interest + maturity.
- **ELSS**: Equity Linked Savings Scheme mutual funds. 3-year lock-in. Dual benefit: investment + deduction.
- **LIC premium**: Premium paid toward life insurance
- **Home loan principal**: Repayment of home loan principal
- **NSC**: National Savings Certificate — 5-year lock-in, fixed rate
- **Children's tuition fees**: Full-time schooling/college fees (up to 2 children)

---

## Section 80D — Health Insurance Deductions

- Individual + family below 60: ₹25,000 deduction on premiums
- Parents below 60: Additional ₹25,000 deduction (total ₹50,000)
- Parents above 60 (senior citizens): Additional ₹50,000 deduction (total ₹75,000)

This makes health insurance for parents a double benefit: protection + tax saving.

## HRA — House Rent Allowance

If you pay rent and receive HRA as part of your salary:

Exempt HRA = Least of:
1. Actual HRA received from employer
2. 50% of basic salary (metro cities) or 40% (non-metro)
3. Actual rent paid minus 10% of basic salary

Maintain rent receipts and landlord PAN (mandatory for rent > ₹1 lakh/year) to claim HRA exemption.

---

## Other Useful Deductions (Old Regime)

**Section 80CCD(1B)**: Additional ₹50,000 deduction for NPS investment (over and above 80C limit).

**Section 80E**: Interest paid on education loan — no upper limit, valid for 8 years.

**Section 80EEA**: Additional ₹1.5 lakh deduction on home loan interest for first-time buyers (conditions apply).

**Section 24(b)**: Interest on home loan deduction — up to ₹2 lakh for self-occupied property.

## Tax-Saving Checklist (Year-Round)

| Month | Action |
|---|---|
| April | Declare investments to employer for reduced TDS |
| April–July | Review health insurance coverage and premiums |
| July | File ITR for previous year (deadline July 31) |
| August–November | Review equity ELSS investments |
| December–January | Maximise PPF contribution if not done |
| January | Submit investment proofs to employer HR |
| February | Purchase LIC/health insurance if needed |
| March 31 | Deadline to make all tax-saving investments for the year |

## Form 16 and ITR Filing

**Form 16**: Issued by your employer. Contains your salary details, TDS deducted, and tax computation. Required for filing ITR.

**Filing ITR**: Mandatory if income exceeds ₹2.5 lakh (old regime). Optional but beneficial if you have multiple income sources or want to claim refunds.

File at incometax.gov.in. Most salaried individuals use ITR-1 (simple form for salary + one house property + other income).

## Common Tax Mistakes to Avoid

- Not declaring freelance or rent income (all income is taxable)
- Missing the ITR deadline (penalty up to ₹10,000 + interest)
- Not verifying Form 26AS before filing (TDS credits must match)
- Over-investing in low-return tax-saving products (traditional LIC plans)
- Ignoring short-term capital gains tax on sold mutual funds/stocks
