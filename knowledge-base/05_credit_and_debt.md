## Credit Cards: Tool, Not Trap

A credit card is a short-term interest-free loan if paid in full by the due date. Used wisely, it provides:
- Reward points, cashback, or airline miles
- Purchase protection and extended warranty
- A 30–50 day interest-free period
- Credit history building (important for future loans)

Used poorly, it becomes expensive debt at 36–48% annual interest.

---

## The Only Rule That Matters

**Pay the full statement balance every month before the due date.**

If you pay only the minimum amount:
- The remaining balance attracts interest at 2.5–4% per month (30–48% per year)
- Interest compounds daily on carry-forward balances
- Your effective cost of purchases balloons rapidly

**Example**: ₹10,000 unpaid balance at 3.5% monthly interest:
- After 1 month: ₹10,350
- After 6 months: ₹12,293
- After 12 months: ₹15,111

A ₹10,000 expense becomes ₹15,111 before you've finished paying it off.

## Credit Score (CIBIL Score)

A CIBIL score (300–900) determines how lenders assess your creditworthiness.

**What affects your score:**
- Payment history (most important — pay on time, always)
- Credit utilisation (keep usage below 30% of your credit limit)
- Credit age (older accounts help)
- Mix of credit types (credit card + personal loan + home loan)
- Hard inquiries (applying for too many loans/cards in short periods lowers score)

**Good range**: 750+ qualifies for lower interest rates on future loans.

## Debt Repayment: Avalanche Method

If you have multiple debts, list them by interest rate (highest first). Pay minimums on all, then attack the highest-rate debt with any extra money.

**Why**: Mathematically optimal — saves the most money in interest.

Example:
1. Credit card: ₹20,000 at 40% → attack first
2. Personal loan: ₹50,000 at 14% → pay minimum until card cleared
3. Education loan: ₹2,00,000 at 8% → pay minimum last

---

## Debt Repayment: Snowball Method

List debts by balance (smallest first). Pay minimums on all, then eliminate the smallest debt first.

**Why**: Psychological wins — clearing a debt entirely is motivating. Behavioural research shows many people stick to snowball better than avalanche.

Choose the method you will actually follow.

## Personal Loans in India

- Typically 11–24% annual interest (varies by lender, credit score, income)
- Often used for emergencies, weddings, home renovation
- Avoid where possible — save in advance instead
- If unavoidable, compare: bank personal loan < NBFC < credit card < payday lender

---

## Buy-Now-Pay-Later (BNPL)

Products like Simpl, LazyPay, or Amazon Pay Later offer short credit lines.
- Interest-free if repaid within the billing cycle
- Become expensive quickly if you carry balances
- Can negatively impact CIBIL score if overdue
- Treat as a convenience tool, not a way to afford things you cannot

---

## Red Flags: Signs of a Debt Problem

- You are spending more than you earn consistently
- You are using one credit card to pay another
- You are borrowing for daily expenses like groceries
- You skip tracking because the numbers are uncomfortable
- Your minimum payment takes more than 10% of your monthly income

If any of these apply, the priority is: stop accumulating new debt, build an emergency fund (even a small one), and create a structured repayment plan.

---

## EMIs and Loan Planning

An EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is a fixed monthly payment for a loan. Before taking an EMI:
1. Calculate total interest paid over the loan tenure (not just monthly amount)
2. Ensure total EMIs ≤ 40% of monthly take-home pay
3. Prioritise closing high-interest EMIs early if you have surplus

EMI calculators are available on BankBazaar, Paisabazaar, and all bank websites.

## Healthy Credit Habits Checklist

- [ ] Pay full credit card balance every statement cycle
- [ ] Keep credit utilisation below 30%
- [ ] Have only 1–2 credit cards (complexity risk)
- [ ] Check CIBIL score annually (free on official site)
- [ ] Never pay only the minimum — it is a trap
- [ ] No EMI for short-lived items (meals, subscriptions, clothing)
