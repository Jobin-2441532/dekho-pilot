## Regulatory Bodies

**RBI — Reserve Bank of India**
Central bank of India. Controls monetary policy, regulates banks, manages exchange rates, and issues currency. All banks must be registered with RBI.

**SEBI — Securities and Exchange Board of India**
Regulates capital markets, stock exchanges, mutual funds, and investment advisors. Ensures investor protection and market integrity. All mutual fund houses and stockbrokers require SEBI registration.

**IRDAI — Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India**
Regulates the insurance industry. Approves products, sets norms for premiums and claims. All insurers must be IRDAI-registered.

**PFRDA — Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority**
Regulates the National Pension System (NPS) and pension funds.

## Banking Terms

**NEFT** (National Electronic Funds Transfer): Fund transfer in batches, settled in 30 minutes, available 24/7. No upper limit. Nominal charges.

**RTGS** (Real Time Gross Settlement): Real-time transfer for large amounts (minimum ₹2 lakh). Instantaneous settlement. Used for high-value transactions.

**IMPS** (Immediate Payment Service): Instant (24/7) fund transfer, up to ₹5 lakh per transaction. Available even on holidays.

**UPI** (Unified Payments Interface): Instant transfers via VPA. No minimum; maximum ₹1–5 lakh per transaction depending on purpose.

**KYC** (Know Your Customer): Process of verifying identity using PAN, Aadhaar, proof of address. Required for opening bank accounts, investing in mutual funds, purchasing insurance.

**IFSC** (Indian Financial System Code): 11-character code identifying a specific bank branch for NEFT/RTGS transfers.

## Investment Terms

**NAV** (Net Asset Value): The per-unit value of a mutual fund. Calculated daily. When you invest in an MF, you buy units at the current NAV.

**AUM** (Assets Under Management): Total market value of assets managed by a fund house. Larger AUM indicates investor trust but does not guarantee better performance.

**Expense Ratio**: Annual fee charged by a mutual fund as a percentage of AUM. Lower is better. Index funds: 0.05–0.5%. Actively managed funds: 0.5–2%.

**SIP** (Systematic Investment Plan): Regular, automatic investment in a mutual fund — monthly, weekly, or daily.

**ELSS** (Equity Linked Savings Scheme): Mutual fund eligible for Section 80C tax deduction. 3-year lock-in. Mix of equity and tax benefit.

**FD** (Fixed Deposit): Bank or NBFC deposit at a guaranteed interest rate for a fixed tenure.

**RD** (Recurring Deposit): Monthly fixed deposit. Commit to regular deposits; bank pays interest on accumulated balance.

**Demat Account**: Dematerialised account that holds shares and securities in electronic form. Like a bank account for stocks. Required for stock market investing.

**Corpus**: Total accumulated amount in a savings or investment pool. "My retirement corpus" means the total money saved for retirement.

**Compounding**: Earning returns on previously earned returns. The longer the period, the more powerful the effect. Einstein reportedly called it the eighth wonder of the world.

## Insurance Terms

**Term Insurance**: Pure life insurance. Pays a large lump sum to nominees if the insured dies during the policy term. No maturity benefit. Cheapest form of life insurance. Recommended for anyone with financial dependants.

**Endowment / Traditional Plans**: Life insurance + savings combined. Returns are low (4–6%). SEBI and financial advisors generally recommend separating insurance (term plan) and investments (MF/ELSS).

**Health Insurance**: Covers hospitalisation costs, surgery, ICU, daycare procedures. Individual or family floater. Essential for all adults.

**Premium**: The periodic payment made to maintain an insurance policy.

**Sum Assured**: The amount the insurer pays upon the occurrence of the insured event (death, hospitalisation, etc.).

## Tax Terms

**PAN** (Permanent Account Number): 10-character alphanumeric identifier for all financial transactions. Required for investing, banking above certain limits, filing income tax.

**ITR** (Income Tax Return): Annual declaration of income and tax paid filed with the Income Tax Department. Due date: July 31 for most individuals.

**Section 80C**: Provides up to ₹1.5 lakh tax deduction for investments in EPF, PPF, ELSS, NSC, LIC premium, home loan principal, children's tuition fees.

**Section 80D**: Tax deduction for health insurance premiums — up to ₹25,000 for self/family; ₹50,000 if parents are senior citizens.

**HRA** (House Rent Allowance): Salary component that allows tax exemption on rent paid, subject to conditions.

**TDS** (Tax Deducted at Source): Tax withheld by the payer before crediting income. Applicable on FD interest, salary, rent above ₹50,000/month, etc.

**Form 26AS**: Annual tax credit statement showing all TDS deducted against your PAN. Available on the Income Tax portal.

## Account Aggregator (AA)

A new RBI-regulated framework that allows individuals to securely and digitally share their financial data (from banks, insurers, mutual fund depositories) with third-party financial service providers — with explicit consent.

Think of it as a data-sharing system where you control who sees your financial information and for how long. Enabled by the RBI's Open Banking vision.

## EMI and Loan Terms

**EMI** (Equated Monthly Instalment): Fixed monthly payment on a loan, combining principal repayment and interest.

**Tenure**: Duration of a loan. Longer tenure = lower EMI but more total interest paid.

**Principal**: The original borrowed amount (excluding interest).

**Moratorium**: Temporary suspension of EMI payments (e.g., during COVID-19). Interest may continue accruing during moratorium.

**Processing Fee**: One-time charge by lender for processing the loan application. Typically 0.5–2% of loan amount.

**Foreclosure**: Paying off the entire outstanding loan before its tenure ends. Banks may charge a prepayment penalty (0–4%).

**CIBIL Score**: Credit score provided by TransUnion CIBIL, ranging from 300–900. Used by lenders to assess creditworthiness.
