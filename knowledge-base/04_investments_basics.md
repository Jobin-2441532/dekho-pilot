## Why Invest?

Savings in a bank account earn 2.5–4% annually. Inflation erodes purchasing power at 4–6% annually. This means money held purely in savings accounts loses real value over time.

Investing allows your money to grow faster than inflation, building long-term wealth.

---

## Mutual Funds

A mutual fund pools money from thousands of investors and invests in stocks, bonds, or both, managed by a professional fund manager.

**Key types:**
- **Equity Mutual Funds**: Invest in stocks. Higher returns (10–15% historically), higher volatility. Suitable for 5+ year horizons.
- **Debt Mutual Funds**: Invest in bonds, government securities. Lower risk (6–8%), stable returns. 1–3 year horizon.
- **Hybrid Funds**: Mix of equity and debt. Moderate risk and return.
- **Liquid Funds**: Ultra short-term, near-zero risk. Better than savings account for parking money.
- **Index Funds**: Track a market index (Nifty 50, Sensex). Low-cost passive investing.

---

## SIP — Systematic Investment Plan

A SIP is an automatic monthly investment into a mutual fund, similar to an EMI but for your own wealth.

**Why SIPs work:**
1. **Rupee cost averaging**: You buy more units when markets are low, fewer when high — averaging out the purchase cost over time.
2. **Discipline**: Automatic deduction means you invest before you can spend.
3. **Compounding**: Returns earn returns. ₹3,000/month at 12% annually for 15 years = ₹15.5 lakhs.

**Starting a SIP**: Minimum ₹500/month on platforms like Zerodha Coin, Groww, Paytm Money, or directly via AMC websites.

## PPF — Public Provident Fund

A government-backed savings scheme:
- **Interest rate**: 7.1% per annum (set quarterly by government)
- **Tax-free**: Interest and maturity amount are tax-exempt under EEE status
- **Lock-in**: 15 years (partial withdrawal allowed after year 6)
- **Annual limit**: ₹500 minimum, ₹1.5 lakh maximum per year
- **Risk**: Sovereign guarantee — zero credit risk

Ideal for: Any Indian wanting safe, tax-free, long-term wealth building.

---

## NPS — National Pension System

A government pension scheme for long-term retirement savings:
- Market-linked returns (mix of equity, corporate bonds, government securities)
- Additional ₹50,000 tax deduction under Section 80CCD(1B) beyond 80C limit
- Lock-in until retirement age (60), partial withdrawal allowed in certain cases

Suitable for disciplined long-term savers who want tax benefits.

---

## FD — Fixed Deposit

A bank or NBFC product where you park money for a fixed term at a guaranteed interest rate.

- Interest: 5.5–8% depending on bank and tenure
- Lock-in: Flexible tenures (7 days to 10 years)
- Penalty for premature withdrawal (typically 0.5–1%)
- TDS applicable: Interest is taxable as per income slab

Best for: Short-to-medium term goals (1–3 years), capital preservation.

---

## Gold

Indians are culturally connected to gold. Financial aspects:
- **Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB)**: Government-issued, 2.5% annual interest + gold price appreciation. No making charges. No risk of theft. 8-year tenure. Best option for gold investment.
- **Gold ETF**: Traded on stock exchange. Lower cost than physical gold. No lock-in.
- **Physical gold**: Illiquid, storage risk, high making charges. Financial products are better.

As a general rule: Limit gold exposure to 5–10% of investment portfolio.

---

## Stocks / Direct Equity

Buying shares in individual companies:
- Highest potential returns, highest volatility
- Requires research, time, and temperament
- Use a SEBI-registered stockbroker (Zerodha, Upstox, ICICI Direct)
- Not recommended as a starting point — mutual funds (especially index funds) are safer for beginners

---

## Key Principles for Indian Investors

1. **Start early**: Time in market matters more than timing the market
2. **Start small**: ₹500/month SIP is a valid beginning
3. **Diversify**: Don't put all money in one asset class
4. **Ignore short-term noise**: Markets go up and down; don't panic-sell
5. **Keep costs low**: Choose index funds with expense ratios < 0.5%
6. **Use tax-advantaged accounts**: PPF, ELSS (80C), NPS (80CCD)
7. **Review annually**: Not monthly — checking too often leads to emotional decisions

---

## The Investment Pyramid (India)

```
         [Stocks]          ← Only 5-10%, once foundation is solid
        [Equity MF]        ← Core wealth builder for 5+ year goals
     [Hybrid / Debt MF]    ← 1-3 year goals
  [FD / RD / PPF / NPS]   ← Guaranteed returns, tax benefits
[Emergency Fund + Savings] ← Always the foundation first
```

Build from the bottom up. Never invest money in equity that you may need within 3 years.
