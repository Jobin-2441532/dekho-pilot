## What Is UPI?

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) is a real-time payment system developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI). It allows instant money transfer between bank accounts via a mobile app — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.

UPI handles over 10 billion transactions per month in India as of 2025, making it one of the largest real-time payment systems in the world.

---

## How UPI Works

- Each UPI user has a VPA (Virtual Payment Address) — e.g., arjun@okhdfc
- You link your bank account to the UPI ID
- To pay: Enter UPI ID or scan QR, enter amount, authenticate with UPI PIN
- Settlement happens in real time between bank accounts — no wallet involved

**Apps**: PhonePe, Google Pay (GPay), BHIM, Paytm, Amazon Pay, WhatsApp Pay, banks' own apps

---

## UPI Limits

- **Default limit**: ₹1 lakh per transaction, ₹2 lakh per day (varies by bank)
- **UPI Lite**: For small payments up to ₹500 — no PIN required, funds stored in device wallet
- Higher limits available for specific purposes (hospital, education): up to ₹5 lakh per transaction
- NEFT and RTGS remain options for very large corporate transfers

## UPI and Spending Behaviour

UPI frictionlessness is a double-edged sword:
- **Pro**: Easy, instant, no cash handling
- **Con**: The ease removes spending friction

Research and anecdotal evidence suggest that digital payment users often underestimate their spending because there is no physical money leaving hands. Transactions happen in background notifications, easy to miss.

**Signs of frictionless overspending via UPI:**
- You are surprised when reviewing your bank statement
- Small amounts (₹50–₹300) appear dozens of times per month
- Zomato, Swiggy, and coffee shop UPI payments dominate your statement

---

## Tracking UPI Spending

PhonePe and Google Pay have in-app expense categorisation and monthly summaries. However, they only show payments — not full context.

For full visibility:
- Download your bank statement (CSV or PDF)
- Filter by UPI transactions
- Categorise manually or use a finance app like Dekho
- Look for frequency patterns — how many transactions per category?

## UPI AutoPay

AutoPay allows recurring payments via UPI — similar to NACH mandates. Examples:
- Netflix, Spotify, mutual fund SIPs, loan EMIs
- Set up once, deducts automatically on schedule
- Can be cancelled anytime through the UPI app

Check your active UPI AutoPay mandates periodically — you may be paying for services you no longer use.

## Safety and Fraud Prevention

**Never share:**
- Your UPI PIN (it is only for authentication — no legitimate bank or service will ask for it)
- OTP received on your phone
- Screenshots of your UPI screen

**Common UPI frauds:**
- "Collect money" requests (scammers send collection requests, not payment links)
- QR codes that are payment requests disguised as receiving codes
- Fake customer care numbers for banks and UPI apps (find official numbers only on the bank's website)
- SIM swap attacks — scammers port your number to a new SIM

**If defrauded:**
1. Call your bank immediately (24/7 helpline)
2. Register complaint on RBI's Sachet portal (sachet.rbi.org.in)
3. File an FIR with cybercrime cell (cybercrime.gov.in)

UPI transactions are irrevocable once sent — prevention is critical.

## BBPS — Bharat Bill Payment System

A centralised platform for paying utility bills — electricity, water, gas, telecom, insurance premiums, school fees, and more.

Accessible via PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, bank apps. All payments under BBPS receive an instant confirmation receipt downloadable for records.

Using BBPS for recurring bills simplifies expense tracking — all bills appear as a single category in your transaction history.

## Rupay vs Visa vs Mastercard

For credit and debit cards in India:
- **Rupay**: India's domestic card network. Works across all Indian ATMs and most merchants. Lower fees, occasionally more merchant offers specific to India. Now accepted internationally.
- **Visa/Mastercard**: International networks, widely accepted globally, essential for international travel.

Most Indians benefit from having one Rupay debit card (for domestic use) and one Visa/Mastercard credit card (for international use and rewards).
